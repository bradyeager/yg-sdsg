How to use this page. Three movement patterns per competition event. Pick the events you want to train, then do all three patterns for that event — or cherry-pick across events. Aim for 4–6 patterns total per session. Warm up first; stop a set short if form breaks down.

Prowler push is out this week — sled is being repaired. It'll return when the sled is back.

KB Box Squat

AMRAP · time-capped
Comp-Pace Goblet AMRAP
01
1 × max reps in 60 sec
Your competition KB, held at chest, at your competition box height
  • This is your event practice. Pace yourself like a comp — don't blow up in the first 15 seconds.
  • Sit fully onto the box, stand all the way up — only the rep that goes all the way down and all the way up counts.
  • Drive through the middle of your foot. Heels stay glued to the floor.
  • If your form breaks (knees collapsing in, chest dumping forward), stop the set early. A bad rep at 70 isn't worth a tweak.
Heavy Goblet Squat
02
3 × 10–20 reps · rest 90 sec between sets
Pick a KB heavy enough that you fail (or barely finish) at 20 reps — usually your competition KB plus 1 or 2 sizes
  • Lower under control — about 2–3 seconds on the way down.
  • Touch the box, stay tight (don't relax onto it), then stand up fast.
  • Hold the KB tight to your chest, elbows tucked in. Don't let it drift forward.
  • Go to or near failure each set. If you cruise to 20 with reps in the tank, go up a KB size next set.
  • If you can't get 10 clean reps, drop a size. The target is the 10–20 range with the last few reps being a grind.
Wall Sit Hold
03
3 × 75–90 sec hold · 60 sec rest between
Bodyweight · thighs parallel to floor (90° at knees and hips)
  • Slide down a wall until your thighs are parallel. Back flat against the wall the whole time.
  • Knees stacked directly over your ankles, not caving in. Feet about hip-width.
  • Hands off your thighs — let them hang at your sides or cross over your chest.
  • Breathe slow through your nose the whole hold. If you're holding your breath, you're hiding from the work.

Dynamax OH Throw

Max reps · 60 sec
OH D-Ball Slam (Comp Practice)
01
4 × 10 reps · rest 60 sec between sets
D-ball at your competition weight (10 lb or 20 lb — 20 lb subs for the 15 lb we don't have)
  • Use a D-ball (slam ball), not a Dynamax ball. The Dynamax bounces wrong for this work.
  • Reach the ball fully overhead — arms straight, ribs tucked down.
  • Slam the ball straight down between your feet. Catch it on the first bounce and go right into the next rep.
  • If the ball is getting away from you or wandering, stop and reset. Don't chase it.
DB Push Press
02
3 × 12 reps · rest 60 sec between sets
DBs that let you finish 12 clean reps — not light, not max
  • Start with DBs at shoulder height, palms facing each other.
  • Small dip at the knees (about 2 inches), then drive the legs straight as you press the DBs overhead.
  • Lock both elbows out at the top — biceps near your ears.
  • Lower the DBs back to your shoulders under control before the next rep.
Plate Overhead Carry
03
3 × 50 meter walk · rest 60 sec between sets
25 or 45 lb plate, held overhead with both hands
  • Press the plate overhead with both hands. Elbows fully locked the whole time.
  • Pull your ribs down toward your hips — don't let your low back arch.
  • Walk steady, normal steps. Eyes forward. Count the steps or measure the distance ahead of time.
  • If your shoulders start to shrug up by your ears, stop and reset before the 50 meters is done.

Bench Press

Max reps @ load
Comp-Load AMRAP Bench
01
3 × max reps · rest 3–4 min between sets
Your competition bench weight, same bar setup as the comp test
  • This is the real test stimulus — go to or near failure each set.
  • First set should be within 2–3 reps of your best. The second and third sets will be lower — that's expected, not failure.
  • Rest the FULL 3–4 minutes. If you cut rest, you're training a different thing.
  • Have a spotter or set up safeties. Don't push yourself under a bar with no plan to get out.
  • Feet planted, glutes tight, slight arch in your low back. Touch the chest every rep — no half reps.
DB Bench Press
02
3 × 15–20 reps · rest 90 sec between sets
DBs that let you finish all 3 sets in the rep range — moderate, not max
  • DBs over your shoulders at the top, palms facing your feet.
  • Lower the DBs to the outside edges of your chest, elbows about 45° from your sides.
  • Press both DBs back up evenly. Don't let one arm lag behind the other.
  • Stop the set when your form starts to break, not when you literally fail a rep.
Push-Ups (AMRAP)
03
3 × max reps · rest 90 sec between sets
Bodyweight — full push-ups, or on your knees, or hands on a bench/box if needed
  • Hands a little wider than shoulders, fingers spread. Squeeze your glutes and brace your core so your body is a straight line from head to heels (or head to knees).
  • Lower until your chest touches the floor. Don't half-rep it.
  • Press all the way back up — elbows fully locked at the top.
  • If full push-ups break form before 10 reps, drop to knees or elevate your hands on a bench.

Overhead Arm Hang

Max hang time
Active Dead Hang
01
3 × hold for time · rest 2 min between sets
Bodyweight, hanging from a pull-up bar. Target each hold around 80% of your best time.
  • Use a step or box to get up to the bar so you don't burn grip jumping up.
  • Grip the bar with thumb wrapped around (hook grip). Don't death-grip — let your skeleton do the holding.
  • Pull your shoulders DOWN toward your back pockets — don't let them shrug up to your ears.
  • Keep your ribs pulled in, body slightly hollow. No swinging.
  • Drop down as soon as your form breaks (shoulders shrugging, grip slipping). Don't fall off.
Scapular Pull-Up
02
3 × 8–10 reps · rest 60 sec between sets
Bodyweight, hanging from a pull-up bar with straight arms
  • Start in a relaxed dead hang — shoulders up by your ears, arms straight.
  • WITHOUT bending your elbows, lift your whole body up an inch or two by squeezing your shoulder blades down and together.
  • Pause 1 second at the top, then lower under control back into the dead hang.
  • If you can't tell whether you're doing it right, watch yourself in a mirror or video the set.
Farmer Carry
03
3 × 60 sec walk · rest 60 sec between sets
A heavy DB or KB in each hand — challenging but you can finish the full 60 seconds with clean posture
  • Pick the weights up like a deadlift — hinge at the hips, flat back, drive the floor away.
  • Stand tall the whole carry. Ribs pulled down over your hips. Shoulders pulled back.
  • Walk steady, normal steps. Don't sprint, don't shuffle.
  • Grip the handles in your fingers, not your palms. Let your skeleton hold the weight — don't crush the grip.
  • Set the weights down with the same hinge you used to pick them up. Don't drop them on the floor.

Med Ball Slams

Max slams · 60 sec
Half-Kneeling Slam
01
4 × 12 each side · rest 60 sec between sets
D-ball at your competition weight
  • Kneel on one knee, other foot flat in front. Front shin straight up and down.
  • Hold the D-ball with both hands, reach it fully overhead — arms straight, ribs tucked.
  • Slam the ball straight down between your front knee and your back knee. Drive through your front hip.
  • Catch on the bounce, reset back overhead, repeat. Switch knees between sets.
  • Use a D-ball only — not a Dynamax ball.
Heavy Standing Slam
02
4 × 6 reps · rest 45 sec between sets
D-ball 1 size heavier than your competition weight (10 lb → 20 lb, 20 lb → 30 lb if available)
  • Stand with feet shoulder-width, ball in both hands at your waist.
  • Pull the ball up over your head — arms fully straight, fully extended through your hips.
  • Use your whole body to slam the ball straight down between your feet. Hips, abs, lats, arms all firing together.
  • Take time to reset between reps. This is for max power per rep, not for the clock.
  • Pick the ball up with a flat back (hinge, not a round-back deadlift).
60-Sec Slam AMRAP (Comp Practice)
03
1 × max reps in 60 sec
D-ball at your competition weight
  • This is the test. Set a timer, set the ball down between your feet, hit start.
  • Pick up, reach fully overhead, slam straight down between your feet. Catch on the bounce. Repeat.
  • Only a rep that goes ALL the way overhead and slams ALL the way down counts.
  • Pace early, finish hard. The last 15 seconds should hurt.
  • If you lose the ball or your form goes ugly, pause, breathe, reset — don't bail entirely.

Jump Rope · 60s

Max singles · 60 sec
Loaded Calf Raises
01
3 × 30 each leg (single-leg) or 3 × 50 (double-leg) · rest 60 sec between sets
Single-leg: a moderate DB held in the same-side hand. Double-leg: a DB in each hand, or a heavy weight vest.
  • Stand on the edge of a step or a sturdy block — heels hanging off the back.
  • Drop your heels BELOW the step to get a full stretch in the calf at the bottom.
  • Push up onto the balls of your feet as high as you can — pause 1 second at the top.
  • Lower under control. No bouncing through the bottom.
  • Pick your variation: do the same one for all 3 sets, or mix (e.g., 2 sets single-leg + 1 set double-leg loaded).
Pogo Hops
02
3 × 30 sec · rest 30 sec between sets
Bodyweight, no rope
  • Feet flat under your hips. Hands at your sides or in front for balance.
  • Hop up and down in place, staying on the balls of your feet. Ankles do the bouncing — knees barely bend.
  • Minimum ground contact time. The instant you land, you're back in the air. Be a spring.
  • If your heels are slapping the floor, you're landing too heavy — go quieter and smaller.
60-Sec Pace Practice
03
2 × 60 sec · rest 90 sec between sets
Real jump rope, sized correctly (handles reach armpits when you stand on the middle of the rope)
  • This is the test. Set a timer for 60 seconds. Single-bounce only (one jump per turn of the rope).
  • Focus on rhythm — about 2 turns per second is a good target. Don't try to max out; smooth wins.
  • If you trip, immediately step back in and keep going. Don't reset the clock.
  • Count out loud or in your head — gives you something to hold rhythm against.

Standing Broad Jump

Longest jump
Box Jump
01
3 × 5 reps · rest 60 sec between sets
Stable box around knee-height. If unsure, go LOWER. A scraped shin is not worth it.
  • Stand about a foot back from the box, feet shoulder-width.
  • Swing your arms back, dip your knees, then explode up and forward onto the box.
  • Land softly — quiet feet, knees bent — fully on top of the box.
  • Stand all the way up on top of the box, THEN step down (one foot at a time). NEVER jump down. Jumping down beats up old knees.
  • Reset all the way before the next rep.
Broad Jump Practice
02
3 × 5 jumps · rest 60 sec between sets
Bodyweight, flat surface with room to land (grass, mat, or a long open floor)
  • Feet shoulder-width behind a line. Swing your arms back as you hinge at the hips and bend the knees.
  • Swing the arms forward as you explode forward — full hip extension at the takeoff.
  • Land on both feet at the same time. Bend your knees to absorb. Stick the landing — no extra hop, no stumble.
  • If you can't stick the landing, you jumped too far for your current strength. Pull it back.
  • Walk back to the start between reps. No rushing.
Hip Hinge to Jump
03
3 × 8 reps · rest 45 sec between sets
Bodyweight
  • Stand tall. Push your hips BACK (like closing a car door with your butt), keeping knees mostly straight.
  • You should feel a stretch in your hamstrings at the bottom — chest tilts forward, back stays flat.
  • Snap the hips forward and up — small jump straight up off the floor. Land soft.
  • This is a HINGE, not a squat. If your knees are bending a lot, you're squatting.
  • The point is to teach the hip to drive the jump. Keep reps crisp — pause and reset if rhythm gets sloppy.

Concept Row · 500m

500m for time
250m Intervals
01
4 × 250m · 1:1 rest (if a 250 takes 60 sec, rest 60 sec)
Pace slightly FASTER than your 500m race pace (about 2–4 sec/500m faster)
  • Set the monitor to "Intervals: distance" and program 4 × 250m with your rest in seconds — it'll beep when to start and stop.
  • Each stroke: drive with the legs first, then swing the back, then pull with the arms. In that order.
  • Recover ALL the way on the slide back. Don't rush the slide — that's where the rest is.
  • Aim for similar splits across all 4 intervals. If the last one is way slower than the first, you went out too hot.
1000m Steady
02
1 × 1000m continuous
Slightly SLOWER than your 500m race pace (about 4–6 sec/500m slower)
  • This is engine work, not a race. Smooth and steady the whole way.
  • Aim for the SAME split every 100m. Look at the monitor every couple strokes.
  • Long, controlled breaths through the nose if possible. Don't pant.
  • If you start fading in the second half, slow down the stroke rate a few beats — keep the power per stroke up.
Power 10s
03
5 rounds · 30 sec hard / 30 sec easy
Hard = all-out, every stroke. Easy = relaxed jog on the rower.
  • On HARD: every single stroke is maximum effort. No coasting.
  • On EASY: actually slow down. The rest is the work — if you can't recover, the next hard interval won't be hard.
  • 5 rounds = 5 hard + 5 easy = 5 minutes total.
  • If you get nauseous, stop. This drill produces lactic acid — that's the point — but don't tip over.

300 Yd Shuttle Run

6 × 50yd for time
50 Yd Shuttle × 6 (Comp Practice)
01
1 set · 6 × 50 yd shuttles · walk back between
Pace slightly UNDER your test speed — about 90%. This is rehearsal, not the test.
  • Set up two lines 50 yards apart. Use cones, chalk, or any landmark you can see.
  • Each shuttle: sprint from line A to line B, plant your foot OVER the far line (not on it), turn, sprint back.
  • Stay low through the turn — bend your knees, drop your hips. Stand up and you'll lose 1–2 seconds per turn.
  • Walk back to the start between shuttles. Let your breath come back, then go again.
  • If a shuttle is way slower than the previous one, you went out too hot. Pace better next time.
100 Yd Build-Up
02
4 × 100 yd · walk back full recovery between
Bodyweight, open grass or track
  • Each 100 yards is a progressive run: first 25 = jog, middle 50 = stride, last 25 = near-sprint.
  • Build the speed smoothly — don't slam the gas at any one point.
  • Walk all the way back to the start between reps. Fully catch your breath.
  • This is for speed and stride mechanics, not conditioning. If you're gasping, you're rushing the rest.
Lateral Shuffle + Sprint
03
3 × 20 yd each direction · rest 60 sec between sets
Bodyweight
  • Set up two cones 20 yards apart. Start at one cone, facing forward (perpendicular to the line between cones).
  • Shuffle laterally to the other cone — stay LOW, knees bent, hips back. Don't bring your feet together; push with the outside foot, recover with the inside foot.
  • At the second cone, turn and sprint straight ahead 20 yards.
  • Walk back, do it again the other direction.
  • 3 each side = 6 total reps. Walk slowly between to keep it crisp.
